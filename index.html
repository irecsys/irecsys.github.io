Recommender Systems

Recommender systems typically produce a list of recommendations tailored to user preferences. They are utilized in a variety of areas including e-commerce, educations, movies, music, news, books, research articles, search queries, social tags, and products in general. Biography

Dr. Yong Zheng is currently a tenure-track Assistant Professor at Department of Information Technology and Management, College of Computing, Illinois Institute of Technology, Chicago, USA. He obtained his PhD degree in computer and information sciences from DePaul University in Chicago. Prior to IIT-Chicago, he worked with Dr. Bamshad Mobasher and Dr. Robin Burke on Web Personalization, Recommender Systems and User Modeling. His research interests lie in Data Science, Personalizations, Recommender Systems, Context-Awareness, Human-Centric Computing, Technology-Enhanced Learning, and so forth. He is active in professional services. He was invited to serve on the organization committee in serveral conferences, such as ACM RecSys, ACM UMAP, ACM IUI, ACM Hypertext. He served on the editoral board for JIIS and IJEntTM. He is a PC member and reviewer for several reputational academic conferences and journals, such as IJCAI, KDD, SIGIR, WWW, RecSys, UMAP, TOIS, TIST, TiiS, TKDE, UMUAI, etc.
